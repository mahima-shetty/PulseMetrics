# Cash Flow Management for Startups

## Runway and Burn Rate
- Runway = Cash in bank / Monthly burn. Aim for at least 18 months before raising.
- Track gross burn (total spend) and net burn (spend minus revenue).
- Cut non-essential costs early; don't wait until the last minute.

## Improving Cash Flow
- **Faster collections**: Invoice immediately, offer early-payment discounts, use automated reminders.
- **Slower payables**: Negotiate net-60 or net-90 with vendors where possible.
- **Subscriptions**: Annual upfront payment improves cash; monthly increases flexibility.
- **Inventory**: If you hold inventory, optimize order quantities and lead times.

## When Cash Is Tight
- Prioritize payroll and critical vendors; defer discretionary spending.
- Consider a line of credit before you need it.
- Renegotiate contracts: many vendors will extend terms if asked.
- Explore revenue-based financing or venture debt as alternatives to equity.

## Red Flags
- Consistently negative operating cash flow without a path to profitability
- High customer concentration or single revenue stream
- Unclear unit economics (CAC, LTV, gross margin)
