# Scaling Operations Without Breaking

## Hire for the Stage
- Early: generalists who can wear multiple hats.
- Growth: specialists who deepen one area (sales, ops, product).
- Avoid over-hiring before you have repeatable processes.

## Systems and Automation
- Document core processes before scaling; otherwise you scale chaos.
- Automate repetitive tasks: invoicing, onboarding, reporting.
- Use tools (CRM, helpdesk, analytics) but avoid tool sprawl.

## Delegation and Ownership
- Clearly define ownership and decision rights.
- Implement regular 1:1s and team reviews.
- Build a culture of "disagree and commit" to move fast without bottlenecks.

## Scaling Challenges
- **Support**: Consider self-serve docs, chat, and tiered support before adding headcount.
- **Sales**: Standardize playbooks and scripts before scaling the team.
- **Engineering**: Technical debt will slow you down; allocate 20% time for refactoring.

## Metrics for Scaling
- Revenue per employee
- Customer satisfaction (NPS, CSAT)
- Time to onboard a new customer or hire
- Operational efficiency (e.g., support tickets per customer)
