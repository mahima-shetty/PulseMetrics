# Product-Market Fit: How to Know When You Have It

## Sean Ellis Test
Ask users: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?"
- If 40%+ say "very disappointed," you likely have strong product-market fit.
- If below 40%, keep iterating on the product and target customer.

## Signs of Product-Market Fit
- Word-of-mouth and organic growth increase
- Customers use the product without being pushed
- Support shifts from "how does it work?" to "when will you add X?"
- Competitors start copying you or talking about you

## Getting to Product-Market Fit
- **Narrow your focus**: Serve one niche exceptionally well before expanding.
- **Obsess over retention**: Retention curves flatten when you have fit.
- **Listen to power users**: They'll tell you what to double down on.
- **Kill features that don't matter**: Simplify; complexity hides fit.

## Common Mistakes
- Chasing too many segments at once
- Building features for the loudest customers instead of the best fit
- Scaling marketing before product-market fit (wastes capital)
- Confusing activity with progress (lots of features ≠ fit)
